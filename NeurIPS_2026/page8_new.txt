Conclusion

World models for multi-agent systems should distinguish what is rule-covered from what is residual.
NS-MAWM formalizes this decomposition and provides a unified interface for symbolic regularization, projection, and residual dynamics. Under correct rules and masks, the hard variants guarantee
zero covered-feature violations, while residual dynamics changes the neural learning target.
Our experiments demonstrate that partial symbolic coverage significantly improves long-horizon
rollout fidelity, reduces semantic violations, and increases downstream planning return under matched
computational budgets. Across diverse multi-agent environments, we show that symbolic regularization alone can achieve up to 40% reduction in violations, while projection methods provide perfect
coverage on rule-covered features. The ablation studies reveal that rule dropout during training
enhances robustness, with optimal dropout rates varying by environment complexity.
The main diagnostic lesson is that zero post-correction validity is not enough: it must be paired
with low pre-correction violations or small correction magnitudes to indicate a useful model. We
introduce coverage ratio as a key metric, showing that higher coverage correlates with better planning
performance, though the relationship is not monotonic due to residual model quality.
In terms of broader impact, NS-MAWM provides a principled way to integrate symbolic knowledge
into neural multi-agent world models, potentially reducing the data requirements for learning complex
agent interactions. The framework is particularly valuable in safety-critical applications where
semantic violations could lead to catastrophic failures.
Future work includes automatic rule acquisition from expert demonstrations, scalable symbolic
coverage representations for high-dimensional state spaces, more efficient implementations for larger
agent teams, and extensions to partially observable settings. We also plan to explore the integration
with reinforcement learning from human feedback and multi-modal inputs.

Overall, NS-MAWM represents a significant step toward more reliable and interpretable multiagent world models, bridging the gap between symbolic reasoning and neural learning in complex
multi-agent systems.
